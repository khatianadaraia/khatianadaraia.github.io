My research examines how social origin shapes the pace of biological ageing, how people’s perceptions of their own social status affect their health, and how trajectories of wellbeing and self-rated health vary across countries in relation to macro-level context. I am currently completing [PhD studies in Social Sciences at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid](https://www.uc3m.es/phdprogram/social-sciences#home){:target="_blank"}. The project grew out of a long-standing interest. Around 2019, I began reading about stress, inflammation, and the psychosocial roots of social stress. I still find this area of research compelling,and it continues to shape my work. More recently, I have been thinking about power: a difficult and often elusive concept, but one that seems central to understanding shame and "the reproduction of the status quo" (disclaimer: nothing Foucauldian). 


I am also interested in building tools that make information retrieval methods and NLP more accessible (democratic).To that end, I am developing [CatEye](https://cat-eye.streamlit.app/){:target="_blank"}, an open-access semantic evidence extraction tool. It is a two-stage neural retrieval engine, combining a bi-encoder with cross-encoder reranking, that maps PDF libraries onto a user-defined conceptual skeleton and returns exact passages with source attribution (non-generative). The current version is an early proof of concept.




Research Interests: Political Sociology;  Stratification;  Quantitative Methods; Health Inequalities
