I am a quantitative social scientist. My research looks at how social origin shapes the pace of biological ageing, how people's perceptions of their own social status affect their health, and how the trajectories of wellbeing and self-rated health vary across countries as a function of macro-level context. I work with large-scale survey data, using longitudinal and panel data analysis methods, including fixed-effects, hybrid models and growth-curve model (among others). 

Currently I am finishing my PhD in Social Sciences at University Carlos III of Madrid (2021-2026). The project grew out of a longer-running interest. Around 2019 I started reading about stress, inflammation, and the psychosocial roots of social stress. I still find this field of research fascinating and continue to work on it. More recently I have been reading about power, an elusive concept, and how it connects to shame and the status quo. Not in the Foucauldian sense. 

If you're researching similar or related topics, don't hesitate to reach out. I'm currently living in Tbilisi and will be here until September (2026), so if you happen to be in this part of the world, get in touch. The odds of someone seeing this GitHub, traveling to Tbilisi, and wanting to connect are admittedly low — but if that's you, please reach out. At the very least we can have a nice chat over coffee or tea.



Research Interests: Political Sociology;  Stratification;  Quantitative Methods; Health Inequalities
