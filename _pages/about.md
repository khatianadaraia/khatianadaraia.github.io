My research examines how social origin shapes the pace of biological ageing, how people’s perceptions of their own social status affect their health, and how trajectories of wellbeing and self-rated health vary across countries in relation to macro-level context. I work with large-scale survey data, using longitudinal and panel data methods,including fixed-effects models, hybrid models, and growth-curve models, among others.

I am currently completing my PhD in Social Sciences at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid.The project grew out of a long-standing interest. Around 2019, I began reading more seriously about stress, inflammation, and the psychosocial roots of social stress. I still find this area of research compelling,and it continues to shape my work. More recently, I have been thinking about power: a difficult and often elusive concept, but one that seems central to understanding shame and "the reproduction of the status quo" (disclaimer: nothing Foucauldian). 


I am also interested in building tools that make information retrieval methods and NLP more accessible (democratic).To that end, I am developing CatEye, an open-access semantic evidence extraction tool. It is a two-stage neural retrieval engine, combining a bi-encoder with cross-encoder reranking, that maps PDF libraries onto a user-defined conceptual skeleton and returns exact passages with source attribution (non-generative). There are similar tools but they are not free.



If you work on similar or related questions, please feel free to get in touch. I am currently based in Tbilisi and will be here until September 2026. So, if you happen to be in this part of the world, do reach out. The chances of someone finding this GitHub page, travelling to Tbilisi, and wanting to meet are admittedly rather slim. But if that happens to be you, I would be very glad to hear from you.




Research Interests: Political Sociology;  Stratification;  Quantitative Methods; Health Inequalities
